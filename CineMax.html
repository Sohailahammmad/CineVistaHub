    AVATAR: THE WAY OF WATER

It is the sequel to Avatar (2009) and the second installment in the Avatar film series. Cast members Sam Worthington, Zoe Saldaña, Stephen Lang, Joel David Moore, CCH Pounder, Giovanni Ribisi, Dileep Rao and Matt Gerald reprise their roles from the original film, with Sigourney Weaver returning in an additional role, while Kate Winslet joined the cast. It follows a blue-skinned humanoid Na'vi named Jake Sully (Worthington) as he and his family, under renewed human threat, seek refuge with the aquatic Metkayina clan of Pandora, a habitable exomoon on which they live.

Cameron said in 2006 that he would like to make sequels to Avatar if it were successful, and he announced the first two sequels in 2010, following the widespread success of the first film, with the first sequel aiming for a 2014 release. However, the addition of two more sequels, for a total of five Avatar films, and the necessity to develop new technology to film performance capture scenes underwater, a feat never accomplished before, led to significant delays to allow the crew more time to work on the writing, preproduction, and visual effects. The filming process, which occurred simultaneously with a currently untitled third film, began in Manhattan Beach, California, on August 15, 2017. The filming location moved to Wellington on September 25, 2017, which ended in late September 2020 after three years of shooting. With an estimated budget of $350–460 million, the film is one of the most expensive films ever made.

"Avatar" stands out as a groundbreaking cinematic masterpiece, with the best 3D technology and stunning visuals that transport audiences to the enchanting world of Pandora. Its breathtaking landscapes, intricate flora, and exotic creatures are a visual marvel. Beyond its eye-catching beauty, the film delves into pressing themes of environmentalism and cultural conflict, making it more than just a visual spectacle. The film's well-developed characters, particularly Jake Sully and Neytiri, add depth and emotional resonance to the story. "Avatar" is an immersive experience that combines its mesmerizing visuals with a thought-provoking narrative, setting a new standard in filmmaking and captivating audiences worldwide.

      JAMES BOND

      STAR WARS

1. A New Hope (Episode IV):

In the innovative galaxy of "A New Hope," young Luke Skywalker embarks on a mesmerizing odyssey alongside the intrepid Princess Leia, charismatic smuggler Han Solo, and the sagacious Obi-Wan Kenobi. The Rebellion's audacious quest against the tyrannical Galactic Empire culminates in a riveting showdown, featuring the revolutionary destruction of the Death Star.

2. The Empire Strikes Back (Episode V):

"The Empire Strikes Back" unfolds with captivating intricacy, as the Rebel Alliance contends with staggering setbacks. Luke Skywalker undergoes mesmerizing training with the enigmatic Yoda, while Han Solo's enthralling capture and a stunning revelation about Darth Vader add layers of depth to this cinematic masterpiece.

3. Return of the Jedi (Episode VI):

The epic "Return of the Jedi" culminates in a spellbinding crescendo as the Rebels orchestrate a final assault against the Empire. This visually stunning climax sees the redemption of Darth Vader and the riveting defeat of the malevolent Emperor, crafting a symphony of heroism and redemption.

4. The Phantom Menace (Episode I):

Episode I, "The Phantom Menace," introduces audiences to a visually resplendent narrative where Jedi Master Qui-Gon Jinn discovers the prodigious Anakin Skywalker amidst a Naboo crisis. The innovative plot foreshadows the impending rise of the insidious Sith, leaving viewers eagerly anticipating the unfolding saga.

5. Attack of the Clones (Episode II):

"Attack of the Clones" immerses audiences in a visually opulent spectacle, depicting the onset of the Clone Wars and the maturation of Anakin Skywalker. A labyrinthine conspiracy unravels, adding layers of complexity to the narrative in this captivating chapter of the Star Wars saga.

6. Revenge of the Sith (Episode III):

Episode III, "Revenge of the Sith," delves into the dark depths of the Force as Anakin succumbs to its seductive power, transforming into the ominous Darth Vader. The birth of the malevolent Galactic Empire unfolds against a backdrop of visually arresting volcanic landscapes and intense lightsaber duels, marking a zenith of innovation in the series.

7. The Force Awakens (Episode VII):

"The Force Awakens" introduces a new era of innovation, blending cutting-edge visual effects with a compelling narrative. The Resistance, led by the indomitable Leia, battles the formidable First Order, while the enigmatic Rey discovers her latent Force abilities, captivating audiences with a fresh, invigorating energy.

8. The Last Jedi (Episode VIII):

Episode VIII, "The Last Jedi," pushes the boundaries of storytelling and visual aesthetics. Rey seeks enlightenment from the reclusive Luke Skywalker, Kylo Ren grapples with conflicting loyalties, and the Resistance fights for survival in a visually stunning symphony that challenges and captivates in equal measure.

9. The Rise of Skywalker (Episode IX):

In the grand finale, "The Rise of Skywalker," the saga reaches a breathtaking zenith. The Resistance confronts the First Order in a visually resplendent clash, Rey's lineage is revealed with a striking narrative twist, and the saga concludes with an emotional and visually spectacular resolution that pays homage to the franchise's rich legacy.

10. Rogue One:

"Rogue One" weaves seamlessly into the original trilogy, employing innovative storytelling and visual effects to craft a riveting tale of sacrifice and heroism. The audacious mission to steal the Death Star plans unfolds against a backdrop of stunning space battles and introduces digital resurrection with the awe-inspiring presence of Grand Moff Tarkin.

11. Solo:

"Solo" explores the early life of the iconic Han Solo with a visual panache that immerses audiences in the gritty, captivating criminal underworld. The narrative unfolds with a swaggering charm, showcasing innovative CGI and practical effects to breathe life into the charismatic scoundrel's formative adventures.

      BLADE RUNNER 2049

"Blade Runner 2049" is visually captivating, boasting breathtaking cinematography and striking production design. Its dystopian future is rendered with meticulous attention to detail, creating a mesmerizing, immersive world. The film's futuristic cityscapes are a fusion of neon-soaked cyberpunk and desolate landscapes, each frame a masterpiece of visual storytelling. The use of color and lighting evokes a haunting atmosphere, enhancing the narrative's noir elements. The stunning special effects and meticulously crafted replicants add depth to the narrative. Every shot is a work of art, making "Blade Runner 2049" a visually stunning and awe-inspiring cinematic experience.

      MISSION: IMPOSSIBLE

"Mission: Impossible" films are a visual spectacle, renowned for their heart-pounding action sequences and breathtaking cinematography. The series consistently delivers jaw-dropping stunts that defy gravity, from Tom Cruise dangling from helicopters to daring high-speed chases. The exotic global locations provide a visually stunning backdrop, from the dizzying heights of Dubai's Burj Khalifa to the elegance of Vienna's opera house. Each installment pushes the boundaries of practical effects and thrilling visual storytelling. The intricate spy gadgets, disguises, and slick choreography add layers of excitement. "Mission: Impossible" captivates with its dynamic visuals, keeping audiences on the edge of their seats.

      PIRATES OF THE CARIBBEAN

"Pirates of the Caribbean" is a swashbuckling adventure franchise that brilliantly combines humor, fantasy, and thrilling action. It's a world where charismatic Captain Jack Sparrow, portrayed by Johnny Depp, steals the show with his eccentric antics and witty one-liners. The series offers a rich, immersive visual experience, with stunning depictions of pirate ships, mystical islands, and supernatural curses. The costumes, makeup, and set design are exceptionally detailed, transporting viewers to a whimsical and treacherous pirate-infested Caribbean. With its memorable characters, memorable music, and a captivating mix of escapades, "Pirates of the Caribbean" remains a beloved and enduring cinematic saga, celebrated for its timeless appeal.

      FAST & FURIOUS

"Fast & Furious" is an adrenaline-fueled film franchise that exhilarates audiences with high-octane car chases, daring stunts, and a focus on family and loyalty. Led by charismatic characters like Dominic Toretto (Vin Diesel) and Brian O'Conner (Paul Walker), it's a celebration of fast cars, epic heists, and gravity-defying action. The series continually pushes the envelope in terms of vehicular mayhem, with each installment offering more outrageous and jaw-dropping sequences. Its global settings, diverse ensemble cast, and a blend of action and emotion have made "Fast & Furious" a long-lasting and thrilling cinematic ride, cherished by fans of fast cars and furious adventures.

      ALITA: BATTLE ANGEL

"Alita: Battle Angel" is a visually stunning sci-fi epic directed by Robert Rodriguez and produced by James Cameron. The film's standout feature is the mesmerizing character Alita, a CGI creation with strikingly realistic eyes and a powerful presence. The dystopian world it presents is a vivid, cyberpunk wonder, rich in imaginative detail. The film seamlessly combines jaw-dropping action sequences with a heartfelt exploration of identity and self-discovery. Alita's journey from amnesiac cyborg to fierce warrior is a compelling narrative. The movie's visual effects, kinetic fight scenes, and memorable characters make it a captivating and visually arresting experience, leaving a lasting impression.

      TITANIC

"Titanic," directed by James Cameron, is a timeless epic that masterfully intertwines romance and disaster. Set against the backdrop of the ill-fated maiden voyage of the RMS Titanic, the film weaves a poignant love story between Jack Dawson (Leonardo DiCaprio) and Rose DeWitt Bukater (Kate Winslet). The visually opulent film immerses viewers in the opulence of the Titanic's grandeur and the harrowing chaos of its tragic sinking. With groundbreaking visual effects, meticulous period detail, and a memorable score by James Horner, "Titanic" remains an emotional and visually captivating masterpiece, continuing to captivate audiences with its breathtaking storytelling and tragic grandeur.

      INCEPTION

Christopher Nolan's "Inception" is a mind-bending masterpiece of the sci-fi genre. The film follows Dom Cobb (Leonardo DiCaprio), a thief skilled in the art of extracting information from dreams. In a world where dreams can be shared and manipulated, the film explores complex layers of reality, blurring the lines between dreams and waking life. Nolan's intricate, visually stunning dreamscapes challenge the audience's perception of reality. The movie's top-tier special effects, gravity-defying action, and a haunting score by Hans Zimmer create an immersive experience. "Inception" is a cinematic puzzle that stimulates the mind while delivering breathtaking visuals, making it a modern classic.

      THE REVENANT

"The Revenant," directed by Alejandro González Iñárritu, is a visual tour de force and a grueling survival tale set against the backdrop of the American wilderness in the early 19th century. Leonardo DiCaprio's portrayal of frontiersman Hugh Glass is riveting, earning him an Oscar. The film's cinematography by Emmanuel Lubezki is breathtaking, capturing the harsh beauty of nature. Iñárritu's signature long takes immerse viewers in the raw brutality of Glass's journey. The natural lighting and realistic bear attack sequence are particularly striking. "The Revenant" is a visceral and visually arresting experience, a testament to the power of storytelling through images.

      TENET

"Tenet," directed by Christopher Nolan, is a mind-bending espionage thriller that delves into the complexities of time manipulation. The film follows the Protagonist (John David Washington) as he navigates a world where time can move both forward and backward. Spectacular action sequences, including a car chase in reverse, are the film's visual highlights, showcasing Nolan's commitment to practical effects. The narrative is layered, demanding active engagement from the audience as it explores the implications of time inversion. Ludwig Göransson's intense score heightens the tension. "Tenet" is a visually stunning, intellectually challenging experience, demonstrating Nolan's mastery of the cinematic medium and his fascination with time.

"Transformers" is an electrifying cinematic masterpiece where giant sentient robots wage an epic battle on Earth.       BULLET TRAIN

"Bullet Train" is a pulse-pounding, action-packed thriller where assassins collide on a high-speed train, delivering a masterclass in innovation and creativity. The movie is a rollercoaster of suspense, humor, and relentless action, with assassins showcasing unique fighting styles and cunning strategies.As each character battles to fulfill their missions, the tension rises, and the stakes get higher. The interplay between Lemon and Tangerine, the dynamic chemistry of the brothers, adds a layer of humor that keeps the audience engaged and entertained.

      THE FATHER

The Bullet Train Kimura's love for his son and desperation to save him drives him onto the bullet train, and he's embroiled in Prince's plot to end The White Death. Kimura encounters many of the assassins on the train, where he is shot by Lemon and confronts both his father, a former Yakuza, and The White Death himself. Kimura is thrust into the film's events unwittingly, but he uses his cunning and expertise to outthink The White Death's henchman when they board the train in the penultimate showdown. While he's as skilled as his father and survives in the end, other assassins have slightly more skill.

      THE ELDER

The Elder makes a late-stage appearance on Bullet Train and leaves a deadly impression. In fact, The Elder has some of the best quotes in the film, displaying his wisdom and patience that suitably fits his nickname. In opposition to The White Death, he reveals himself as a dutiful grandfather, whose always one step ahead. The Elder, a former Japanese Yakuza, uses a katana against various henchmen and appears to be an incredibly swift and resourceful fighter. He outsmarts The Prince at her own game, revealing he goes to all lengths to protect his own. He duels The White Death and maintains his robustness against him in their ultimate showdown among the train rubble. His experience is unwavering as he thoroughly and carefully plans and prepares his vengeance, which in the end, he succeeds.

      THE LADYBUG

Brad Pitt's turn as Ladybug is a welcome one, as he walks the train both stealthy and in confusingly good spirits. As an assassin who was displaced onto his mission - as a replacement of sorts - he shows incredible skill to overcome any worthy opponent. Through the many high energy fight scenes, Ladybug holds his own against the best of the best.

Ladybug's good at his job, and that's clear in the way he outplays all the other assassins in pursuit of the briefcase. The interesting thing about him is that he's very reluctant about what he does for a living, yet his so-called "bad luck" has been anything but bad. He would rather be anywhere else than on his current mission to avoid the toxic environment that comes as a hitman. More importantly, as a person who avoids guns, Ladybug survives this long without using one, which showcases his capability to triumph over others with his sharpness and fighting combat. As a cherry on top, Pitt gives Ladybug a refreshing attitude as a deadly assassin with an everyman attitude.

      TANGERINE

Aaron Taylor Johnson's British assassin boards the bullet train with a suave demeanor that rivals James Bond. He and his brother not- so-carefully guard the silver briefcase filled with cash, but it's their chemistry fans loved about Bullet Train. As an assassin, though, he can als fend for himself. Tangerine is ruthless in his skill as an assassin, nearly meeting his match in Brad Pitt's Ladybug. They engage in various bloody, messy bouts of hand-to-hand combat - the first resulting in a stalemate and the second resulting in Tangerine's demise. While he's incredibly talented and loves his brother, he's unwittingly shot in the neck, earning him a lower ranking.

      LEMON

Lemon works as a hitman with his brother Tangerine, and they step on the bullet train with The Son and a silver briefcase filled with cash. As an accomplished actor with many great movies and TV shows, Brian Tyree Henry infuses Lemon with humor and heart, with little quirks, such as how he compares everyone he meets to characters in Thomas The Tank Engine. Lemon and Tangerine work in tandem, but it is clear Lemon is the brains, and Tangerine is the brawn of their operation. He's able to grapple any situation against the other assassins, first succumbing to a drugged water bottle from Ladybug, then is saved from a fatal bullet by a bulletproof vest. He isn't shown to have as much skill as an assassin as the others on the train - besides shooting Kimura - but his survival due to his cunning and common sense earns him a higher ranking.

      THE SON

Logan Lerman plays the White Death's son, whose appearance sets the chain reaction to introduce audiences to the much more skilled assassins, Tangerine and Lemon. His brotherly bodyguards are tasked with escorting him and the money-filled briefcase to the gang of assassins they work with.

      THE PRINCE

The Prince begins the film in the train's first-class section, coercing Kimura to join her after blackmailing him with the death of his young son, who she put in the hospital. She enlists his help to kill The White Death and spends the film manipulating many other assassins.

What The Prince lacks in muscle, she makes up for in mind, as she's shown to be a master manipulator. Her innocent schoolgirl appearance helps her in her master plan to kill The White Death, as she schemes and turns various assassins against one another. She's adept at making bombs, as she places them in a gun and the briefcase that ultimately stops her Russian kingpin father.

      THE WOLF

Bady Bunny's Mexican assassin has a good reason for wanting revenge on the train. Shown in a flashback, the audience sees his family and bride killed at his wedding, and he boards the train to exact revenge. Unfortunately, for The Wolf, he has a run in with Ladybug attempting to exit. While he's similar to other characters in action thrillers like Bullet Train, the Wolf is extremely skilled as an assassin, nearly beating Ladybug at his own game. He may be ruthless, as his past says a lot about his marksmanship and observant abilities but doesn't last very long when he's outsmarted and stabbed with his own weapon. His martial-arts fighting style serves him well, and his efficacy with a weapon is impressive, but he's lower ranked for how quickly he was killed off.

      THE HORNET

Using boomslang poison to incapacitate her enemies, The Hornet's trademark killing style is seen early on in the film, but she isn't revealed as the culprit until the film's second act. Hiding behind a disguise for much of the film, she reveals herself to Ladybug and tries to kill him. The Hornet possesses much skill in the way she evades notice for much of the film and dons different disguises until she's ready to attack her targets. She's ruthless and lacks remorse for the horrid way she kills her victims, but she's not the most skilled assassin of the way Ladybug outmaneuvers her with her poison.

      THE WHITE DEATH

Mostly shown in flashbacks until his third-act reveal, The White Death paints a menacing picture for the audience. Flashbacks reveal the Russian assassin infiltrated the Japanese criminal underworld and took it by force. He masterminded the film's entire operation, pressuring the assassins onto the train to kill one another.

Michael Shannon's filmography is long and impressive, and he fully embodies the Russian crime boss with malice and mystery. The White Death is shown as an unstoppable force and superior intellect, cautiously coordinating every move to bring all the assassins he holds ill-will. He showcases this skill to kill when he battles Ladybug, The Elder, and Kimura right after the other. Despite being a brutal and wicked assassin, he only ends up dead due to The Prince's booby-trapped weapon that he did not see coming.

      CARVER

Carver is an assassin who targeted the White Death's wife and was initially hired by him to retrieve his son's briefcase on the bullet train, but got replaced by Ladybug.
